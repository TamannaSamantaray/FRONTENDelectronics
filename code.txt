  <div class="row" id="Theory">
    <h4>THEORY:</h4><br>

    <div class="fwd-bias" id="fwd-id">
    <h6>1.A diode is a device having two terminals the p(anode) and n(cathode) in which current flow in only one direction in which. </h6><br>
    <h6>2.In an n-type material, the electron is the majority carrier and the hole is the minority carrier. In p-type material, the hole is the majority carrier and electron is the minority carrier.</h6> <br>
    <h6>3.Biasing refers to the application of an external voltage across the two terminals of the device to extract a response.</h6><br>
  </div>

    <h4 >Forward bias Condition:</h4><br>
    <div class="fwd-bias" id="fwd-id">
    <h6>1.A forward bias condition is achieved when positive potential is applied to p-type material and the negative potential is applied to n-type material.</h6><br>
    <h6>2.The forward bias potential VD will provide pressure to the electrons in n-type materials and holes in the p-type materials which result in the recombination of holes and electrons with ions near the boundary and the width of the depletion region is reduced.</h6><br>
    <h6>3.As a result, number of immobile ions (acceptor and donor ions) in the depletion region decreases.</h6><br>
    <h6>4.So, depletion width and junction potential reduce.</h6><br>
    </div>
    <div class="container-fluid">
      <div class="row" style="justify-content: center;">

    <p style="text-align: center;">
      fig.(1)Forward Biased P-N Junction Diode
    </p>
    <h4>Reverse bias Condition:</h4><br>
    <div class="fwd-bias" id="fwd-id">
    <h6>1.A reversed bias condition is achieved when an external potential is applied across the p-n junction such that the positive terminal is connected to the n-type whereas negative terminal is connected to p-type material.</h6><br>
    <h6>2.The total number of uncovered positive ions present inside the depletion region of the n-type material will increase because of the greater number of free electrons drained to the positive potential of applied voltage.</h6> <br>
    <h6>3.The number of uncovered negative ions will increase in the p-type material due to the same reason and the depletion region will be increased as the net result.</h6><br>  
    <h6>4.As the reverse biased voltage rises, the strength of the depletion region electric field also rises. The p-n junction depletion zone begins to breakdown if the electric field intensity rises over a critical point, and current starts to flow typically through either the Zener breakdown process or the avalanche breakdown process.</h6> <br>
  </div>

   <p>For the forward bias and reverse bias, the general characteristics of a diode can be defined by an equation known as Shockley’s equation:</p> 
   
   <span style="font-size: 1rem;">
    <p style="float:right;">(1.1)</p>
      $$ {I_D = I_S(e^\frac{V_D}{nV_T})} $$                             
   </span>


   <br>
   <br>
   <span style="font-size: 1rem;">
    <p style="float:right;">(1.1)</p>
    $$ {I\ =\ \frac{V_{s1-V_{s2}}}{R_4}} $$                             
  </span>

   <span style="font-size: 1rem;">
    <p style="float:right;">(1.1)</p>
    $$ {\ V_T = \frac{KT_K}{q} } $$                             
   </span>
