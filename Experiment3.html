THEORY:


1.Rectification is the method by which an alternating current which is periodically in reversed direction, is changed into a direct current.

2.Rectification uses the unidirectional conduction feature of the semiconductor diode.

3.A rectifier is an electrical device used for this process of conversion.

4.A rectifier can be classified into two types: Half-wave Rectifier and Full-wave Rectifier. The half-wave rectifier is explained below.

HALF-WAVE RECTIFIER:


1.The half-wave rectifier is the most basic type of rectifier circuit.

2.Only a portion of an input signal can flow through a half-wave rectifier circuit. The circuit consists of a single diode connected in series with a resistor, which serves as a load.

Positive Half Cycle:

fig.(1)Positive Half Cycle

1.During the positive half-cycle of the input, the diode is forward biased and conducts. A voltage is developed across the load as the current flows through it.

Negative Half Cycle:

fig.(2)Negative Half Cycle

1.During the negative half-cycle of the input, the diode is reversed biased and it does not conduct.

2.Therefore, as no voltage appears across the load resistor in the negative half-cycle of the input there is no flows of current in the load resistor.

3.Thus, only for the first half of the cycle, the dc voltage across the load is sinusoidal and a pure ac input signal is transformed into a pulsing dc output signal.

FULL-WAVE RECTIFIER:


1.Similar to a half-wave rectifier, a full-wave rectifier enables unidirectional current to flow through the load over the entire sinusoidal cycle (as opposed to only half the cycle in the half-wave).

2.Because the current through the load is unidirectional, the voltage that is generated across the load throughout both half cycles is also unidirectional.

3.A full-wave rectifier transforms the entire input waveform into an output waveform with constant polarity (positive or negative).

CENTRE-TAPPED RECTIFIER:

1.In a centre-tapped full-wave rectifier, the diode (D1) is forward biased and switched on and the diode (D2) is reverse biased and switched off during the positive half of the input voltage (VS). A positive output voltage is produced by the output resistance and the current through (D1).

2.Now, during the negative half cycle, (D1) is switched off and reverse biased and (D2) is switched on forward biased, this causes the current flowing through the output resistance to once again result in a positive output voltage.

1. What is a rectifier circuit, and what is its purpose?

Ans. A rectifier circuit is an electronic circuit that converts an alternating current (AC) signal into a direct current (DC) signal. The purpose of a rectifier circuit is to provide a steady and constant flow of current in one direction for electronic devices that require DC power.

2. What is a diode, and how does it work as a rectifier?

Ans. A diode is an electronic component that allows current to flow in one direction and blocks it in the opposite direction. In a rectifier circuit, a diode is used to convert the AC signal into a DC signal by allowing only the positive half of the AC signal to pass through while blocking the negative half.

3. What is the efficiency of a rectifier circuit, and how can it be calculated?

Ans. The efficiency of a rectifier circuit is the ratio of the DC power output to the AC power input. It can be calculated by dividing the DC output power by the AC input power and multiplying the result by 100 to get the percentage efficiency.
