THEORY:


1.A diode is a device having two terminals the p(anode) and n(cathode) in which current flow in only one direction in which.

2.In an n-type material, the electron is the majority carrier and the hole is the minority carrier. In p-type material, the hole is the majority carrier and electron is the minority carrier.

3.Biasing refers to the application of an external voltage across the two terminals of the device to extract a response.

Forward bias Condition:


1.A forward bias condition is achieved when positive potential is applied to p-type material and the negative potential is applied to n-type material.

2.The forward bias potential VD will provide pressure to the electrons in n-type materials and holes in the p-type materials which result in the recombination of holes and electrons with ions near the boundary and the width of the depletion region is reduced.

3.As a result, number of immobile ions (acceptor and donor ions) in the depletion region decreases.

4.So, depletion width and junction potential reduce.

fig.(1)Forward Biased P-N Junction Diode

Reverse bias Condition:


1.A reversed bias condition is achieved when an external potential is applied across the p-n junction such that the positive terminal is connected to the n-type whereas negative terminal is connected to p-type material.

2.The total number of uncovered positive ions present inside the depletion region of the n-type material will increase because of the greater number of free electrons drained to the positive potential of applied voltage.

3.The number of uncovered negative ions will increase in the p-type material due to the same reason and the depletion region will be increased as the net result.

4.As the reverse biased voltage rises, the strength of the depletion region electric field also rises. The p-n junction depletion zone begins to breakdown if the electric field intensity rises over a critical point, and current starts to flow typically through either the Zener breakdown process or the avalanche breakdown process.

fig.(2)Reversed Biased P-N Junction Diode

For the forward bias and reverse bias, the general characteristics of a diode can be defined by an equation known as Shockley’s equation:
