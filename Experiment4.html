THEORY:


1.The term clipper refers to a network that uses diodes to clip away a section of an input signal without altering the remaining portion of the applied waveform.

2.The positive or negative region of the signal is clipped off depending on the orientation of the diode.

3.Clippers are generally categorised into two types: series and shunt. In series configuration the diode is in series with the load and in the parallel configuration the diode is in a branch parallel with the load.

SERIES CLIPPER CIRCUIT::


1.A clipper is referred to be a series clipper if the output is taken across the resistor and the diode and resistor are connected in series with the input AC (voltage) signal.

2.We can clip either the positive or the negative half of the input signal depending on the location of the diode.

3.Based on this, series clipper circuits are divided into two types: Positive Series Clipper and Negative Series Clipper.

Positive Series Clipper:

If a series clipper clips the positive half of the input signal, it is considered to be a positive series clipper.

fig.(1)

Negative Series Clipper:

If a series clipper clips the negative half of the input signal, it is considered to be a negative series clipper.

SHUNT CLIPPER CIRCUIT:


1.A clipper is referred to as a shunt clipper if the output is taken across the diode and the resistor and diode are connected in series with the input AC (voltage) signal.

2.We can clip either the positive or the negative half of the input signal depending on the location of the diode.

3.Based on this, shunt clipper circuits are divided into two types: Positive Shunt Clipper and Negative Shunt Clipper.

Positive Shunt Clipper:

If a shunt clipper clips the positive half of the input signal, it is considered to be a positive shunt clipper.

1. What is a clipper circuit, and how does it differ from a clamper circuit?

Ans.A clipper circuit is an electronic circuit that clips or cuts off a portion of an input signal, while a clamper circuit shifts the DC level of the input signal without changing its shape.

2.How can the output waveform of a clipper circuit be controlled?

Ans.The output waveform of a clipper circuit can be controlled by adjusting the amplitude and phase of the input signal, the values of the circuit components, and the polarity of the diode. By changing these parameters, the level at which the input signal is clipped and the shape of the output waveform can be modified.
